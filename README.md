# DeFi_CeFi_Dependency_Analysis
## Executive Summary
This repository hosts a multi-stage research project investigating the decoupling (or lack thereof) between Decentralized Finance (DeFi) and Centralized Finance (CeFi). By constructing a high-fidelity dataset spanning 2017–2025, this study quantifies how macroeconomic stress—defined by interest rates, CPI deviations, and labor market shocks—propagates into DeFi liquidity.

## Project Architecture
The research is organized into two distinct phases:
**1. Phase 1: Statistics for Finance (Preliminary Study)**
Focuses on *classification and signal detection*. By binarizing DeFi TVL growth, this phase identifies which CeFi "stressors" (e.g., NAIRU breaches or CPI spikes) are the most reliable predictors of a DeFi liquidity contraction.
**2. Phase 2: Master’s Thesis (In Progress)**
Focuses on *magnitude and dynamic dependencies*. Utilizing a continuous DV (Log Returns of TVL), this phase applies advanced Deep Learning (LSTM) and structural econometrics to measure the elasticity of DeFi responses to TradFi shocks.
